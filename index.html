DON'T DECK YOUR PHONE

This game reads motion sensors, but physics still wins. A bad catch can destroy a phone or hurt someone.

  • Practice over a bed, couch, or thick carpet.
  • Use a protective case and remove dangling cables.
  • Stay clear of faces, pets, lights, fans, and breakable objects.
  • Start with small hand flips before attempting real tosses.

SET UP MATCH

Name the players. The phone will tell you whose turn it is.

HOW IT READS A TRICK

Roll around the phone's long edge becomes kickflips or heelflips. Flip end-over-end becomes frontflips or backflips. Spin while screen-up becomes shove-its. Mixed axes become combo tricks.

Tap ARM THROW, hold steady for a moment, then toss. The app ignores handling movement and only counts an attempt after it sees a launch, real airtime, and a separate catch or settled landing.

Heading behavior: while the phone is in your hand, only compass heading follows you so the screen side stays pointed toward you. Pitch and roll remain real. A detected throw freezes heading until the catch.

Browser note: if motion events are blocked from a local file, serve this same HTML over HTTPS or use Trick Lab to test every game mode.
